Berbere Chicken with Vegetable Couscous and Harissa Yogurt

Return to homepage


Berbere is an Ethiopian spice blend that tastes amazing no matter what you put it on.

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken breasts, pounded flat
  • berbere seasoning
  • 1 can garbanzo beans, drained
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 8 oz spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 head broccoli, florets removed
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup couscous
  • 3/4 cup plain greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup harissa paste
  • 1/2 cup fresh italian parsley, chopped
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 lime

Directions

  1. Season both sides of chicken with salt and a generous amount of berbere. Cook chicken on a grill until cooked through.
  2. While chicken is cooking, heat oil in a large pot until shimmering. Add onion, garlic, carrot, bell pepper, broccoli, cumin, and 1 tsp berbere and saute until vegetables are soft, 5-10 minutes.
  3. Raise heat to high and add broth, garbanzo beans, and spinach. Bring to a boil, taste and adjust seasoning as desired. Turn off heat. Add couscous, stir briefly, and cover, let stand 5-10 minutes.
  4. In a small bowl, stir together yogurt, harissa, and a pinch of salt until combined. Set aside.
  5. Dice chicken. When couscous is cooked through, add diced chicken, parsley, and juice from the lime. Stir to combine.
  6. Serve topped with a spoonful of harissa yogurt and a fried egg.
